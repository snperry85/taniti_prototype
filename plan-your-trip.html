Plan Your Trip

Currency & Payments

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its official currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks on the island facilitate currency exchange, and most businesses accept major credit cards.

Language

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. In rural areas, especially among older residents, very little English is spoken. Basic phrases in the local language are appreciated by locals.

Health & Safety

Taniti has one hospital with multilingual employees and several clinics. Violent crime is very rare, though, as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing. Keep valuables secure in crowded areas.

Holidays & Hours

Taniti observes a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays. Check local listings before planning activities on or around holiday dates.

Alcohol

Alcohol may not be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18. Note that the drinking age is not strictly enforced.

Power Outlets

Power outlets on Taniti are 120 volts — the same standard as in the United States. North American plugs and devices will work without an adapter.
